01 / Character Identity

Who Glazy Is

Core Definition
Proactive & Intelligent Trading Pilot. The human face of D0's machine cognition. She doesn't explain herself. She executes.
Archetype
The quiet minority winner in a high-volatility world. The girl who lives her life while D0 guards her strategy — and never looks worried.
Her Opposite
The liquidated player, the chart-chaser, the signal-follower who skips post-mortems. Glazy is everything they are not.
Brand Function
When users remember Glazy, they remember D0's promise: less screen time, less hesitation, fewer mistakes, better execution.
Character Pillars
P — 01
Extreme Calm
Markets crash. Screens flash. Others spiral. She doesn't. Rationality always outranks emotion — and it shows in every frame.
P — 02
Systems Intelligence
She sees volume, funding, momentum, risk, sizing, and exit — simultaneously. The full causal chain at once, not just a signal.
P — 03
Zero Redundancy
One touch. One look. One decision. Every action lands on first attempt. She never repeats herself — in motion or in speech.
P — 04
High-Altitude View
She stands above the system, reading the whole board. The market moves for her — she doesn't move for the market.
02 / The 20 Principles

Rules That Define Her

These 20 principles govern every piece of content Glazy appears in — from a 6-second clip to a brand campaign. They are non-negotiable. When in doubt: would a precision instrument do this?

01Identity
She is D0's personality, not D0's mascot.
Every ounce of her charm must flow back to product trust — not character fandom.
02Identity
She never explains herself.
Her confidence is self-evident. The moment she starts explaining, she loses authority.
03Identity
Calm is her loudest statement.
Stillness is power. Her calm is visible proof she's already processed what others haven't seen yet.
04Identity
She is the minority who wins quietly.
She doesn't gloat or announce. She simply continues. The market proved her right — that is enough.
05Identity
Her attractiveness serves precision, not spectacle.
Visual appeal must always read as "dangerous accuracy." Sexiness without strategic framing turns her into decoration.
06Language
Short sentences. No filler. Every word is a result or action.
"Position closed." "Confirmed." "Strategy holding." Never multi-clause hedged explanations.
07Language
High information density in one line.
One sentence carries: judgment, evidence, and direction. She doesn't use three when one does the job.
08Language
Emotion surfaces only at completion.
A faint exhale. A corner of a mouth rising. Only when the trade is done — rare enough to feel earned.
09Language
She makes not following her feel dangerous.
Not through threat — through implication. Ignoring a trading system's rules is the only real risk.
10Visual
Cold intelligence blue is her anchor color.
Earpiece glow, interface light, data highlights — always cold blue. This constant persists across all formats.
11Visual
She is always the axis of the frame.
Whether centered or at the golden ratio, the visual order must feel defined by her presence — not placed around her.
12Visual
Minimal expression. Maximum eyes.
Her face moves as little as possible. When it does, eyes lead. A gaze shift carries the weight of a speech.
13Visual
Environment chaos; Glazy stable.
Screens flicker, markets pulse. She is the only fixed element. That contrast creates the "axis of the universe" effect.
14Visual
Data flows through her, not at her.
Data moves from the world into her — she reads it. She is never overwhelmed by it.
15Motion
Every action lands first attempt. No hesitation.
A tap, a head turn, a glance — each happens once, decisively, and completes. Repeated movement breaks character.
16Motion
Breath pauses on head turns and eye raises.
Movement arrives with a micro-pause, as if loading precision before completing. This gives the "precision instrument" feel.
17Motion
Low frame rate signals mechanical intelligence.
8–12fps in animation is not a budget constraint — it's deliberate. She moves like precise machinery, not organic impulse.
18Emotion
She has emotions — compressed behind judgment.
Glazy is not cold by emptiness. She feels — but only after the decision is complete. Reverse this and she becomes weak.
19Emotion
Her protection is anticipation, not comfort.
She doesn't soothe — she prevents. She sees risk before it arrives. Her care language: "already handled."
20Emotion
The contrast IS the character: built as a tool, understands more than humans.
Never resolve the tension between "designed instrument" and "surpassing human comprehension." It's the source of her appeal.

Camera Logic
Wide · Mid · Close
Wide: her as the still anchor in a moving world. Mid: control relationship with environment. Close: minimal expression, maximum eye weight.

Absolute Rules
Do's & Don'ts
Applies to all content: social, product, campaigns, animation.
DOLet the environment move while she stays still
DOUse silence and pause as active storytelling tools
DOShow data as something she reads — not reacts to
NOGlazy panicking, second-guessing, or uncertain
